His bottle of champagne was
produced in the cellars of the
urbana Wine Company, Inc., Ham
mondsport, N. y., under the personal
supervision of Monsieur Charles
Fournier, a Frenchman who mastered
the art of wine making in the caves
of Veuve Clicquot Company at Reims,
France. There he received his train
ing and served as Chief wine maker
and Assistant General Manager from
1926 until 1934. Since the spring of
1934, Monsieur Fournier has been
associated with the urbana Wine Com
pany, Inc.
CThis wine, available in limited
quantities, is very dry, light in body
and color, with a most delicate bou
quet that proclaims it a masterpiece of
skillful blending. Monsieur Fournier
is indeed proud of his product, and
feels confident that the American con
noisseur will instantly recognize in it.
the inherent qualities of a truly great
champagne.
